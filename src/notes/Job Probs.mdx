How do you get a job?

You don't need to land an offer for every single job you apply to. *You need to get just one*. The moment you have your first success, you can stop interviewing.


$$
P(\text{zero offers}) = (1-p)^n
$$

What is the probability you get *at least one* offer after $n$ applications?

$$
P(\text{at least one offer}) = 1-(1-p)^n
$$

So, there are two ways you can increase your chances:

- Make $p$ go up: become a more qualified candidate, apply for roles that match your experience etc
- Make $n$ go up: apply and interview at more places

What does this tell us?

$p$ is more tune-able, and should be the bulk of your effort. You can become much more qualified by learning fundamentals, doing practice interviews, and aligning your profile with the ideal candidate's.

But $n$ is also a variable! And pretty simple to keep investing in. So don't give up. Keep applying.


Caveating that we made some simplifying assumptions:
- That each application is independent, and doesn't affect future outcomes
- That each job is equally attainable
